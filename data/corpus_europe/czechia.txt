Czechia - Country Information\n==================================================\n\n## Introduction\nBackground:\n  By terms of the 1960 Treaty of Establishment that created the independent Republic of Cyprus, the UK retained full sovereignty and jurisdiction over two areas of almost 254 square kilometers -- Akrotiri and Dhekelia. The larger of these is the Dhekelia Sovereign Base Area, which is also referred to as the Eastern Sovereign Base Area.\n\n## Geography\nLocation:\n  Eastern Mediterranean, on the southeast coast of Cyprus near Famagusta\nGeographic Coordinates:\n  34 59 N, 33 45 E\nMap References:\n  Middle East\nArea:\n  Total:\n    131 sq km\n  Note: note: area surrounds three Cypriot enclaves\nArea - Comparative:\n  about three-quarters the size of Washington, DC\nLand Boundaries:\n  Total:\n    108 km\n  Border Countries:\n    Cyprus 108 km\nCoastline:\n  27.5 km\nClimate:\n  temperate; Mediterranean with hot, dry summers and cool winters\nGeography - Note:\n  British extraterritorial rights also extended to several small off-post sites scattered across Cyprus; several small Cypriot enclaves exist within the Sovereign Base Area (SBA); of the SBA land, 60% is privately owned and farmed, 20% is owned by the Ministry of Defense, and 20% is SBA Crown land\n\n## People And Society\nPopulation:\n  approximately 15,500 on the Sovereign Base Areas of Akrotiri and Dhekelia including 9,700 Cypriots and 5,800 Service and UK-based contract personnel and dependents\nLanguages:\n  Languages:\n    English, Greek\n  Major-Language Sample(S):\n    Το Παγκόσμιο Βιβλίο Δεδομένων, η απαραίτητη πηγή βασικών πληροφοριών.
